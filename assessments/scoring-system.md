## ðŸ“ Regional Scoring Criteria

### Cervical Alignment (0-10)

**10 - Perfect**
- Head directly over shoulders
- Neutral cervical curve
- No forward head posture
- Occipital-wall distance: 0-2 cm

**7-9 - Good**
- Minimal forward head posture
- Occipital-wall distance: 3-5 cm
- Can easily correct to neutral

**4-6 - Fair**
- Moderate forward head posture
- Occipital-wall distance: 6-10 cm
- Difficulty maintaining correction
- Occasional neck tension

**1-3 - Poor**
- Severe forward head posture
- Occipital-wall distance: >10 cm
- Cannot touch head to wall
- Chronic pain/headaches

**0 - Critical**
- Extreme protrusion
- Severe pain
- Neurological symptoms

### Scapular Position (0-10)

**10 - Perfect**
- Scapulae flat against ribcage
- No winging
- Symmetric position
- Proper upward rotation

**7-9 - Good**
- Minimal protrusion
- Slight asymmetry
- Good control during movement

**4-6 - Fair**
- Moderate protrusion/winging
- Visible asymmetry
- Limited scapular control

**1-3 - Poor**
- Severe winging
- Marked protrusion
- No scapular control
- Pain with overhead movement

**0 - Critical**
- Complete dysfunction
- Severe pain
- Unable to use shoulder

### Thoracic Curve (0-10)

**10 - Perfect**
- Normal kyphosis (20-40Â°)
- Good extension mobility
- No stiffness

**7-9 - Good**
- Slightly increased curve (40-50Â°)
- Some extension available
- Minimal stiffness

**4-6 - Fair**
- Moderate hyperkyphosis (50-60Â°)
- Limited extension
- Visible rounding

**1-3 - Poor**
- Severe hyperkyphosis (>60Â°)
- Rigid, no extension
- "Hunchback" appearance

**0 - Critical**
- Structural deformity
- Severe pain
- Pathological curve

### Lumbar Alignment (0-10)

**10 - Perfect**
- Normal lordosis
- Neutral pelvis
- Good flexibility

**7-9 - Good**
- Slight deviation from neutral
- Mild anterior/posterior tilt
- Can self-correct

**4-6 - Fair**
- Moderate lordosis or flat back
- Noticeable pelvic tilt
- Limited correction ability

**1-3 - Poor**
- Severe hyperlordosis or flat
- Chronic low back pain
- Cannot find neutral

**0 - Critical**
- Structural pathology
- Severe pain
- Disability

### Core Stability (0-10)

**10 - Perfect**
- Can maintain plank >90 sec
- Excellent anti-extension control
- No compensation

**7-9 - Good**
- Plank 60-90 sec
- Good control
- Minimal compensation

**4-6 - Fair**
- Plank 30-60 sec
- Moderate control
- Some compensation

**1-3 - Poor**
- Plank <30 sec
- Poor control
- Significant compensation

**0 - Critical**
- Unable to perform
- No control
- Pain with activation

### Body Symmetry (0-10)

**10 - Perfect**
- Perfect bilateral symmetry
- No shoulder/hip height difference
- Balanced muscle development

**7-9 - Good**
- Minor asymmetry (<1 cm)
- Barely noticeable
- No functional impact

**4-6 - Fair**
- Moderate asymmetry (1-2 cm)
- Visible difference
- Some functional compensation

**1-3 - Poor**
- Severe asymmetry (>2 cm)
- Obvious deformity
- Significant compensation

**0 - Critical**
- Extreme asymmetry
- Structural scoliosis
- Major dysfunction

## ðŸ“ˆ Progress Tracking

### Minimum Clinically Important Difference (MCID)
- **1.0 point improvement** = Noticeable subjective change
- **2.0 points improvement** = Significant objective change
- **3.0+ points improvement** = Major transformation

### Expected Progression Rates

**With High Compliance (90%+):**
- **Weeks 0-4:** +0.5-1.0 points
- **Weeks 4-8:** +1.0-1.5 points
- **Weeks 8-12:** +0.5-1.0 points
- **Weeks 12-24:** +0.5-1.0 points

**With Moderate Compliance (70-90%):**
- Expect 50-75% of above rates

**With Low Compliance (<70%):**
- Minimal to no improvement
