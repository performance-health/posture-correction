### 1. Visual Observation

- [ ] Cervical curve (lordosis)

#### Posterior View Checklist

- [ ] Spine alignment (scoliosis screening)
- [ ] Scapular symmetry
- [ ] Gluteal fold symmetry
- [ ] Calf muscle bulk

### 2. Quantitative Measurements

#### Wall Test
**Purpose:** Measure forward head posture severity

**Procedure:**
1. Stand with back against wall
2. Heels touching wall
3. Attempt to touch: buttocks, shoulder blades, head
4. Measure distance from wall to back of head (occiput)

**Scoring:**
- 0-2 cm: Normal
- 3-5 cm: Mild FHP
- 6-10 cm: Moderate FHP
- >10 cm: Severe FHP

#### Thoracic Extension Test
**Purpose:** Assess thoracic spine mobility

**Procedure:**
1. Lie supine with foam roller under thoracic spine
2. Arms overhead, attempt to touch floor
3. Measure distance or note restriction

#### Chin Tuck Endurance
**Purpose:** Test deep neck flexor strength

**Procedure:**
1. Supine position, neutral spine
2. Perform chin tuck (head lift 2-3 cm)
3. Hold position, count time or reps until fatigue

**Scoring:**
- <10 sec: Poor
- 10-20 sec: Fair
- 20-30 sec: Good
- >30 sec: Excellent

### 3. Functional Movement Screening

#### Overhead Reach
- Can raise arms overhead without arching back?
- Scapular control maintained?

#### Squat Pattern
- Can squat to parallel without excessive forward lean?
- Heels stay down?
- Neutral spine maintained?

#### Single Leg Balance
- Can maintain 30+ seconds each side?
- Stable pelvis?

## üìù Documentation

### Essential Records
1. **Photos:** Date-stamped, consistent angles
2. **Measurements:** All quantitative data
3. **Subjective:** Pain, discomfort, limitations
4. **Notes:** Observations, compensations, concerns

### Progress Tracking Schedule
- **Initial:** Baseline complete assessment
- **Week 4:** Quick visual check + measurements
- **Week 8:** Photos + key measurements
- **Week 12:** Full re-assessment
- **Every 3 months:** Comprehensive evaluation
